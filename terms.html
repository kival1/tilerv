     The tiler agreement is a document that defines the conditions and requirements that will apply during the tiler's work, as well as the rights and obligations of the customer and the tiler.
Key points that may be included in a tiler agreement may include the following:
    Terms of payment: the agreement may determine the cost of work, terms and methods of payment.
Obligations of the tiler: The agreement may define the obligations of the tiler, including the timing of the work, the materials and tools used, and the quality of the work.
   
     Obligations of the customer: The agreement may define the obligations of the customer, including the provision of access to the premises, the preparation of surfaces on which the tiles will be installed, and payment for labor.
   
     Warranties: The agreement may specify warranties for work performed and materials used.
Terms of termination of the agreement: The agreement may determine the terms of termination of the agreement, including terms and procedures for notification.
     
    Confidentiality: The agreement may define the terms of confidentiality, including the protection of personal data and trade secrets.
Dispute Resolution: The agreement may define procedures for resolving disputes and claims arising in the course of the work.
     A tiler agreement may be in writing and signed by both parties to ensure agreement and understanding of the terms and conditions.
